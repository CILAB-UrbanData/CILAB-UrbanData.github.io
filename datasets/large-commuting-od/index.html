LargeCommutingOD

LargeCommutingOD is a large-scale commuting origin–destination (OD) dataset that integrates data from the U.S. Census Bureau (commuting flows, sociodemographics, administrative boundaries) and OpenStreetMap (points of interest). It provides standardized region-level attributes and OD matrices for 3,233 areas across the United States, covering diverse metropolitan, town, and rural environments.

Dataset Description

This extensive dataset captures commuting origin–destination (OD) flow patterns across the entire United States, spanning 3,233 areas that use counties and metropolitan regions as administrative boundaries. It offers a unified, nationwide view of how residents commute between where they live and where they work.

Each region is characterized by a rich set of 131 urban attributes, including sociodemographic indicators (population structure, income, education, household and vehicle ownership, commuting time, etc.) derived from the American Community Survey (ACS), as well as 34 categories of points of interest (POIs) from OpenStreetMap. The dataset also provides pairwise centroid distances between regions and commuting OD matrices aggregated from the 2018 LODES dataset. All records are structurally standardized for research while preserving statistical integrity.

This dataset is particularly valuable for studies in urban mobility modeling, commuting behavior analysis, transportation planning, and machine learning applications focused on OD flow prediction and generation, as well as benchmarking new generative and graph-based models.

LargeCommutingOD is organized into multiple files describing region-level attributes and commuting OD matrices. The exact file structure may vary slightly by subset; please refer to the official repository for complete details. Below is a representative schema for the main components.
